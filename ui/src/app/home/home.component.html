Event Storming is an exercise for the team to get together walking though the product to identify all command/events and define an ubiquitous language within the subdomain.

Two main focus of event storming are

  • Focus On Behavior
  • Place Events on Timeline

Participants, including business stakeholders, software engineers and other stakeholders or subject matter experts, get together to model the application with Commands and Events and put into a timeline.

Key terms:

  • Bounded Contexts
    • Consider human culture and interaction
    • Changes in ubiquitous language
    • Strongly separated bounded contexts will result in smooth workflows
  • Commands
    • Blue sticky note
    • Represents a request to perform an action
    • The action has not yet happened and i can be rejected
    • Usually to a specific destinations
    • Causes a change to the state of the domain
  • Events
    • Orange sticky note
    • An action that happened in the past (past tense) that your domain experts care about
    • The action could be a step in business process, something that happens on a scheduled basis, something meaningful that occurs as a result of something else happening.
    • Since it’s already completed, they can’t be rejected
    • Bostcast to many destinations
    • Record a change to the state of the domain. Often the result of a command.
